📖 Foundations of Higher Mathematics: A Detailed Walkthrough

Comprehensive, example-driven introduction to Functions (and their special families), Precalculus, Calculus I & II, Matrices (inverses, eigenvalues, applications), and Basic Statistics. No shortcuts — each concept includes rigorous definitions, derivations, real-world applications, and extended examples.

📌 1. The Concept of Functions: Heart of Mathematical Modeling

🔹 Formal Definition: A function \( f : X \to Y \) is a relation that assigns to each element \( x \) in the domain \( X \) exactly one element \( y = f(x) \) in the codomain \( Y \). The set of all outputs is the range.
Notation: \( y = f(x) \), \( x \) is independent variable, \( y \) dependent.
Vertical line test: A graph represents a function if any vertical line intersects it at most once.

Functions encode dependencies: population over time, cost vs. quantity, temperature distribution. Key operations: composition \( (f \circ g)(x) = f(g(x)) \), inverse functions (if \( f \) is bijective), and transformations (shifts, stretches).

✨ Special Function Families — Detailed Breakdown

📈 Linear Functions
General form: \( f(x) = mx + b \) (slope \( m \), y-intercept \( b \)).
Rate of change: constant \( m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} \).
Example: Distance traveled at constant speed: \( d(t) = 70t \) miles (m=70).
Application: Cost models \( C(x) = 12x + 150 \) (variable + fixed cost).
Inverse: \( f^{-1}(y) = (y-b)/m \) (if \( m \neq 0 \)).
🌀 Quadratic Functions
\( f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c \), \( a \neq 0 \). Parabola opens up if \( a>0 \). Vertex \( x_v = -\frac{b}{2a} \).
Example: Projectile motion: \( h(t) = -4.9t^2 + 30t + 2 \) (height in meters). Max height at \( t = 30/(9.8) \approx 3.06s \).
Discriminant: \( \Delta = b^2-4ac \) determines roots.
Applications: Profit maximization, area optimization, satellite dishes.
⚡ Exponential Functions
\( f(x) = a \cdot b^{x} \) where \( b > 0 \), \( b \neq 1 \). Growth if \( b > 1 \), decay if \( 0 < b < 1 \).
Natural exponential: \( f(x)=e^{kx} \). Doubling time \( T_2 = \frac{\ln 2}{k} \) (for growth).
Example: Investment: \( A = 1000 e^{0.05t} \), after 10 years \( A \approx 1648.72 \).
Applications: Radioactive decay (half-life), population growth, epidemiology.
📊 Logarithmic Functions
\( y = \log_b(x) \) ⇔ \( b^y = x \) with \( x>0 \). Natural log: \( \ln(x) = \log_e(x) \).
Properties: \( \log_b(mn) = \log_b m + \log_b n \), \( \log_b(m^k)=k\log_b m \).
Example: \( \log_2(64)=6 \).
Applications: pH = \(-\log_{10}[H^+]\), Richter scale, sound intensity (decibels).
🌊 Trigonometric Functions
Sine, cosine, tangent: periodic with period \( 2\pi \) (sin, cos). \( \sin^2 x + \cos^2 x = 1 \).
Example: \( y = 5\sin(3x-\pi/2) \): amplitude 5, period \( 2\pi/3 \), phase shift \( \pi/6 \).
Applications: AC circuits, harmonic oscillators, Fourier series, navigation.
🧩 Polynomial & Rational
Polynomial: \( P(x) = a_n x^n + ... + a_0 \). Rational: \( R(x) = P(x)/Q(x) \).
Example: \( f(x)=\frac{x^2-1}{x-2} \) has vertical asymptote at \( x=2 \).
Applications: Curve fitting, electrical impedance, control theory.
📚 Detailed Example – Composition & Inverses: Let \( f(x)=3x+2 \) and \( g(x)=x^2 \). Then \( (f \circ g)(x) = 3x^2+2 \), \( (g \circ f)(x) = (3x+2)^2 \). Inverse of \( f \): \( f^{-1}(y) = (y-2)/3 \). In real life, converting Celsius to Fahrenheit then to Kelvin is composition.

📐 2. Precalculus: Preparing for Change

Precalculus solidifies function analysis, introduces limits informally, and explores advanced algebraic concepts: polynomial long division, partial fractions, parametric equations, polar coordinates, and sequences/series.

🔹 Binomial Theorem: \( (x+y)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} x^{n-k}y^k \). Used in probability and combinatorics.
🔹 Trigonometric Identities: \( \sin(2\theta)=2\sin\theta\cos\theta \), \( \cos(2\theta)=\cos^2\theta-\sin^2\theta \).
🔹 Exponential-logarithmic inversion: \( \ln(e^x)=x \), \( e^{\ln x}=x \) for \( x>0 \).
🌍 Real-world precalculus application – Logistic growth: \( P(t)=\frac{K}{1+Ae^{-rt}} \) models population with carrying capacity \( K \). For \( K=1000, A=4, r=0.3 \), half capacity reached at \( t = \frac{\ln A}{r} \approx 4.62 \) time units.

⚡ 3. Calculus I: Derivatives – The Instantaneous Rate

Derivative definition: \( f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \) (if the limit exists). Geometrically: slope of tangent line. Physically: velocity if \( f \) is position.
Differentiation Rules
• Power rule: \( \frac{d}{dx}x^n = n x^{n-1} \)
• Product rule: \( (fg)' = f'g + fg' \)
• Quotient rule: \( \left(\frac{f}{g}\right)' = \frac{f'g - fg'}{g^2} \)
• Chain rule: \( \frac{d}{dx}f(g(x)) = f'(g(x)) g'(x) \)
• \( \frac{d}{dx}\sin x = \cos x \), \( \frac{d}{dx}e^x = e^x \), \( \frac{d}{dx}\ln x = 1/x \).
Applications: Optimization & Related Rates
Critical points: solve \( f'(x)=0 \) to find local extrema.
Example – Minimize material: Cylindrical can volume \( V=\pi r^2 h = 500 \) cm³. Surface area \( S=2\pi r^2+2\pi r h = 2\pi r^2 + 1000/r \). \( S'(r)=4\pi r - 1000/r^2 =0 \) → \( r^3 = 250/\pi \) → \( r \approx 4.30 \) cm, \( h\approx 8.60 \) cm.
Related rates: A balloon inflating: \( \frac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi r^2 \frac{dr}{dt} \).

📦 4. Calculus II: Integrals – Accumulation of Quantities

Definite integral: \( \int_a^b f(x)\,dx = \lim_{n\to\infty} \sum_{i=1}^n f(x_i^*)\Delta x \). Represents signed area under \( f \) from \( a \) to \( b \).
Fundamental Theorem of Calculus: If \( F'(x)=f(x) \), then \( \int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b)-F(a) \). Integration reverses differentiation.
Integration Techniques
• Power rule: \( \int x^n dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}+C \) (\( n\neq -1 \))
• Substitution: \( \int f(g(x))g'(x)dx = \int f(u)du \)
• Integration by parts: \( \int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du \)
• Partial fractions for rational functions
• \( \int \frac{1}{x}dx = \ln|x|+C \)
Applications: Area, Volume, Work
Area between curves: \( \int_a^b [f(x)-g(x)]dx \).
Volume of revolution (disk method): \( V = \pi \int_a^b [f(x)]^2 dx \).
Work: \( W = \int F(x)\,dx \) (variable force).
Example: \( \int_0^3 x^2 dx = [x^3/3]_0^3 = 9 \).
Probability: For a continuous random variable, \( P(a\le X\le b)=\int_a^b f_X(x)dx \).

🧮 5. Matrices: Inverses, Eigenvalues & Eigenspaces

Matrix: rectangular array of numbers. \( A_{m\times n} \). Square matrix \( n\times n \). Identity \( I_n \).
Inverse: If \( AB = BA = I \), then \( B = A^{-1} \). Exists iff \( \det(A) \neq 0 \). Used to solve \( A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b} \).
Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors: Scalar \( \lambda \) and nonzero vector \( \mathbf{v} \) satisfy \( A\mathbf{v} = \lambda \mathbf{v} \). Compute via \( \det(A-\lambda I)=0 \) (characteristic polynomial).
🔢 2×2 Inverse Formula
For \( A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \), \( \det(A)=ad-bc \). If \( \det \neq 0 \):
\[ A^{-1} = \frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix} \]
Example: \( A=\begin{bmatrix}4&2\\1&3\end{bmatrix} \), \( \det=10 \), \( A^{-1}=\frac{1}{10}\begin{bmatrix}3&-2\\-1&4\end{bmatrix} \).
✨ Eigenvalue Computation
Solve \( \det(A-\lambda I)=0 \). For 2×2: \( \lambda^2 - \text{trace}(A)\lambda + \det(A)=0 \).
Example: \( A=\begin{bmatrix}2&1\\1&2\end{bmatrix} \), trace=4, det=3 → \( \lambda^2-4\lambda+3=0 \) → \( \lambda=3,1 \). Eigenvectors: for \( \lambda=3 \), solve \( (A-3I)v=0 \) → \( v=(1,1) \).
Applications: Principal Component Analysis (PCA): eigenvectors of covariance matrix give principal directions. Google PageRank uses dominant eigenvector.

📊 6. Basic Statistics: Describing & Interpreting Data

Descriptive statistics: Summarize data using measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and spread (variance, standard deviation, IQR).
Mean: \( \bar{x} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n x_i \).
Variance: \( s^2 = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n (x_i-\bar{x})^2 \) (population formula). Standard deviation \( s = \sqrt{s^2} \).
Normal Distribution & Z-scores
\( X \sim N(\mu,\sigma^2) \): bell curve. \( Z = \frac{X-\mu}{\sigma} \). Empirical rule: ≈68% within 1σ, 95% within 2σ, 99.7% within 3σ.
Application: IQ scores ~ N(100,15). A score of 130 corresponds to \( Z = (130-100)/15=2 \) → 97.7th percentile.
Sampling & Correlation
Correlation coefficient r: measures linear association between two variables (\( -1 \le r \le 1 \)).
Simple linear regression: \( \hat{y} = b_0 + b_1 x \).
Example: Height vs. weight: strong positive correlation (r ~ 0.7). Used in predictive modeling.
📉 Detailed statistical example: Dataset: [4, 8, 6, 5, 3]. Mean = (4+8+6+5+3)/5 = 26/5 = 5.2. Variance = [(4-5.2)²+(8-5.2)²+(6-5.2)²+(5-5.2)²+(3-5.2)²]/5 = [1.44+7.84+0.64+0.04+4.84]/5 = 14.8/5 = 2.96. Standard deviation ≈1.72. Median = 5. Applications: quality control, A/B testing, economics.
💡 Summary & Intellectual Bridge: Functions describe dependencies; calculus measures change and accumulation; matrices handle multi-dimensional transformations and systems; statistics extracts insight from data. Together they form the quantitative language of science, engineering, finance, and AI.
